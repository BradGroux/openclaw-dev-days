# End-to-End Sample Scenario

## Scenario
A workshop team wants to build **NeighborFit**, a lightweight app that helps people discover local walking routes, track consistency, and unlock simple motivation badges.

The goal is not to build a full fitness platform. The goal is to show how a small startup team can use specialized AI agents to move from idea to demo.

## Step 1 — Market Researcher
### Prompt
Research products adjacent to NeighborFit. Identify direct competitors, adjacent products, and patterns we should study for motivation loops, progress tracking, and simple onboarding.

### Good Output Should Include
- products like Strava, AllTrails, Nike Run Club, Finch, and habit apps
- common patterns around streaks, achievements, progress indicators, and route discovery
- recommendations for what to borrow and what to avoid in a workshop MVP

## Step 2 — Idea Validator
### Prompt
Pressure-test NeighborFit as a workshop MVP. Clarify the target user, challenge weak assumptions, and cut this to the smallest useful version.

### Good Output Should Include
- target user: casual walkers who want lightweight motivation, not hardcore runners
- narrowed MVP: route list, walk history, progress bar, badge unlocks
- cuts: social feed, advanced map intelligence, full training plans
- a tighter value proposition

## Step 3 — Project Manager
### Prompt
Turn the validated NeighborFit concept into a 90-minute workshop execution plan with milestones, task order, and the next immediate step.

### Good Output Should Include
- milestone 1: scaffold app shell and sample data
- milestone 2: route cards and walk tracking
- milestone 3: progress and badge unlock display
- milestone 4: polish the hero, onboarding, and demo narrative

## Step 4 — Coder
### Prompt
Build the thinnest useful version of NeighborFit based on the validated MVP and project plan. Prioritize a coherent demo over completeness.

### Good Output Should Include
- a working app shell
- route list or cards
- progress state and reward treatment
- enough polish to feel like a product, not a raw scaffold

## Step 5 — Outreach Assistant
### Prompt
Write launch/demo/recap language for NeighborFit. Explain what the app does, who it is for, and why the MVP is compelling.

### Good Output Should Include
- a crisp product summary
- social/demo copy
- a short workshop recap
- CTA ideas such as trying the demo or iterating the concept further

## Why This Scenario Works
This scenario is small enough for a workshop but rich enough to show:
- research
- validation
- planning
- implementation
- messaging

It teaches attendees that AI works better when each agent has a defined role instead of one giant vague prompt.
